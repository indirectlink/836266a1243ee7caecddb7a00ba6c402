Face masks can be re-used, provided they still appear to be in good working condition, and have been stored long enough for any viral particles on them to have become inactive.
By James Daniel Marrs Ritchey
Published on January 4, 2025
Quotes
You can re-wear a mask after you have stored it in a paper bag for a few days
waiting for, say, five to seven days should be enough time for it to be inactivated.
Getting the mask wet or agitating the mask with soap can damage the material.
If your mask is wet due to condensation from breathing, you can reuse it.
A mask is still wearable if its elastic bands continue to create a secure fit and the material looks clean and provides good airflow
